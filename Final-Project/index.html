Bibliography

The Queen, Elizabeth Alexandra Mary of the United Kingdom was born on April 21, 1926 in Mayfair, London. She was the first child of The Duke and Duchess of York. The Queen gained a sister in 1930, Princess Margret Rose. She was married to her distant cousin Philip Mountbatten of the Royal Navy for 73 years. They had four children, Pince Charles, Princess Anne, Prince Andrew, and Prince Edward Mountbatten-Windsor, eight grandchildren and eleven great-grandchildren Her coronation was held on June 2, 1953 in Westminitser Abbey

Early Life

Princess Eliabeth spend most of her childhood Royal Lodge in Windsor Great Park until the coronation of her late father, King George VI. When her father succeeded to the throne in 1936, Princess Elizabeth became the first in line to the throne and started stduying constitutional history and law, in prepartion for her future. She was enrolled in art, music, swimming, horse-riding, and French classes.

Monarchy

When Princess Elizabeth was born, no one, even her family, expected that one day she would become Monarch, the longest-reigning Monarch The Queen surpassed Queen Victoria in 2015 to become the longest-reigning monarchy in 2015. She took reign in February 6, 1952 and became the Head of the CommonWealth and Queen of the United Kingdom and Northern Ireland.
